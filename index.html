Who are we?

If you are a radical scientist in Canada who understands that our present institutions serve to legitimize capitalist ideology and advance the material interests of the ruling class, you may feel isolated at your place of work– but you are not alone! If you, like us, want to meet other like-minded scientists with whom to learn and struggle, we want to be friends (and comrades).

Science for the People (SftP) Canada seeks to build a network of radical scientists in Canada who seek to learn and work in the anti-capitalist tradition, comprising feminist, anti-imperialist, and anti-racist class struggles. Our network enables us to share our personal experiences in mainstream STEM, make sense of these experiences collectively, develop anti-capitalist analyses, and put radical theory into practice. Get involved to meet others who are interested in organizing, learning together, and supporting one another in class struggle.

SftP first emerged in 1969 from the anti-war movement in the US and provided an organization through which to demand that science be subordinated to the needs of the people rather than corporate greed and imperialism. In 2015, a new generation of scientists revitalized SftP with the support of many of its original members.

This Canadian chapter, formed in 2022, reflects the growing struggles that radical scientists from coast to coast are engaged in everyday. Radical scientists are everywhere, young and old, inside and outside of establishment institutions- but we need to organize to find one another. Join us, and let’s get to know one another!

Ways to get involved.

SftP publishes a flagship magazine that provides commentary and analysis on wide-ranging topics: everything from radical science as resistance in places like Palestine and the Philippines, to anti-imperialist frameworks for combatting the climate crisis. Through our network, we organize reading and study groups, support one another in understanding and navigating our experiences in mainstream institutions, join international struggles, and work to better understand the insidious ideological assumptions that are legitimized by basic science in the Canadian context. Our organizing starts with building relationships with one another and allows us to act collectively against systems of oppression in Canada. There’s plenty of work to be done both within establishment institutions as well as on the streets: supporting Indigenous land defenders from Grassy Narrows to Fairy Creek, opposing imperialist Canadian mining investments in the global south, and organizing engineers to refuse the building of new fighter jets, to name a few.

To get in touch, tell us a bit about yourself and indicate how you would best like to get involved. Whether you want to write for the publication, build local branches, get involved in direct actions, or just study and connect with other comrades, we want to meet you! And if you don’t know how you would like to participate, no worries! We will send you an invite to our next meeting all the same, where you can meet fellow members and learn more about our current and future work.
